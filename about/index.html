Daniel Simcoe was raised in Southern California and moved to Oregon to raise a family, and has lived in Oregon for over 30 years.

Mr. Simcoe received his Undergraduate Degree at San Diego State University in 1972. Thereafter, he obtained his Juris Doctorate from McGeorge School of Law in 1980. He studied Constitutional law with Professor Anthony Kennedy, who later became a Justice of the United States Supreme Court.

Mr. Simcoe is licensed to practice law in Oregon and California, as well as the United States federal courts. He has also received specialized training in Mediation.

Our staff is committed to giving clients the assistance they need in a timely way. We return telephone calls promptly and encourage potential clients to schedule an appointment to discuss their concerns.

After speaking with you, I can provide you with a recommendation about your best course of action, an estimate of the cost of your issue, and a calculation of the time it will take to accomplish your goals. I recognize that each case is unique and that every problem has a different solution. I tailor my representation solely to you and your needs.
